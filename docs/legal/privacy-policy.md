# Privacy Policy

Version dated 2026-04-21

Mad Thinker, Inc. ("Mad Thinker," "we," "our," or "us") operates the Mad Thinker platform at www.madthinker.ai and the EmeraldWatersAnglers mobile application (together, the "Service"). This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, share, and protect information when you use the Service.

If you do not agree with this Policy, do not use the Service.

## 1. How You Access the Service

You may access the Service through one of three paths. Your path affects who sees the data you submit.

- **Through a lodge or business customer.** A lodge, outfitter, or similar business licenses the Service from Mad Thinker and provisions access for its guides and anglers using a community code. When you access the Service this way, your lodge can see your catch reports, photos, voice notes, location data, and submitted observations associated with trips you conduct or participate in with them.
- **Through a conservation agency.** A conservation or research agency licenses the Service and provisions access for researchers. When you access the Service this way, your agency can see all data you submit. Agencies may also see submissions from guides at partner lodges who have opted in to share conservation data.
- **Directly as a public user.** You download the Service from the App Store and sign up without a community code. You may use a free tier or a paid subscription. Only you (and parties you explicitly share with) see your data.

## 2. Information We Collect

### Information you provide directly

- **Account information:** name, email address, password, user role (angler, guide, researcher, public), community code (if applicable), and member ID.
- **Profile information:** optional demographic details (age, role, certifications, experience) you provide to personalize your use of the Service.
- **Support communications:** information you provide when you contact us for help.

### Information the mobile app collects when you use it

- **Photos:** photos you capture or upload, including catch photos, fish head photos, and associated metadata (time, device orientation, EXIF). Photos are stored on your device and, for submitted reports, uploaded to our servers.
- **Precise location (GPS):** latitude and longitude attached to catch reports, observations, and activity records. Collected only when you submit a report; not collected in the background.
- **Voice recordings:** audio captured when you record a field note or voice memo, plus an on-device transcript.
- **Machine-learning inference results:** our on-device models analyze photos you capture and produce estimates of species, fish length, fish sex, and a confidence score. These estimates, plus a 26-feature measurement vector derived from your photo, are uploaded with your catch report. **These estimates are AI-generated predictions, not authoritative identifications.**
- **Device and diagnostic information:** device model, operating system version, app version, crash logs, and performance data.
- **Authentication data:** if you enable Face ID or Touch ID, your biometric data is handled entirely by your device's secure enclave and is never transmitted to us.

### Information collected automatically

When you use the Service, we and our service providers automatically collect usage information, including session timing, features used, and anonymized interaction patterns. On the website, we use cookies and similar technologies as described below.

### Information from third parties

If you access the Service through a lodge or conservation agency, that organization may provide us with your name, email, role, and pre-paid license information before you create your account.

## 3. How We Use Information

We use information to:

- Provide, operate, and support the Service;
- Authenticate you and secure your account;
- Run on-device machine-learning models to estimate species, length, and other catch characteristics;
- Share your submissions with your lodge or conservation agency if you access the Service through one, as described in Section 1;
- Enable community features (feeds, maps, shared trips) with users you're connected to;
- Aggregate and anonymize data for conservation research, fisheries management, species population studies, and product improvement;
- Respond to your support requests and communicate administrative messages;
- Detect, investigate, and prevent fraud, abuse, and violations of our Acceptable Use Policy;
- Comply with legal obligations and enforce our agreements.

We do not sell your personal information. We do not use your catch data, photos, location data, or voice notes for behavioral advertising.

## 4. How We Share Information

### With your lodge or conservation agency

If you access the Service through a lodge or conservation agency, that organization acts as the controller of data you submit on their community. They can view and export your catch reports, photos, voice notes, location data, and trip records. Their use of your data is governed by the agreement between them and Mad Thinker and their own privacy practices. Contact them directly for questions about how they use your data.

### With other users in your community

Content is visible to other users only in the ways described below. There are no public or cross-community feeds in the current version of the Service.

- **Lodge or agency administrators** can view and export all catch reports, photos, voice notes, location data, and trip records submitted by users they have provisioned in their community.
- **On shared trips,** the guide and the angler(s) assigned to a trip can view each other's catch reports from that trip.
- **Other guides in your lodge community** can view anonymized catch and non-catch data submitted by guides in the same community — species, location, length, time, and aggregated counts — with no photos and no linkage to the guide or angler who submitted the data. This feature helps guides benefit from local knowledge without exposing individual patterns.
- **Public users and researchers** can see only their own submissions.

### With service providers

We use third-party service providers who process data on our behalf under contracts that restrict their use of your data:

- **Supabase** (United States) — authentication, database, file storage. Stores your account, catch reports, photos, voice notes, and associated metadata.
- **Mapbox** (United States) — map tiles and geocoding. Receives coarse location data needed to render maps.
- **Apple** (United States and per Apple's infrastructure) — App Store distribution, In-App Purchase processing for public subscriptions, and on-device services (Face ID, push notifications). Apple handles payment information for IAP; we do not receive card details.
- **Website analytics** — Google Analytics on our marketing website only. Not used within the mobile app.
- **On-device machine learning** — MediaPipe (Apache 2.0, Google) and Apple Core ML. These models run entirely on your device; your photos are not transmitted to any third party for ML processing.

### For research and conservation

If you are a guide who has opted in to conservation sharing, or a researcher accessing the Service through a conservation agency, your submissions (including species, location, length, photos, and associated metadata) may be shared with the agency and with its research partners. We may also use aggregated and anonymized data — data that cannot reasonably be used to identify you — for fisheries research, academic publication, population modeling, conservation reporting, and product improvement. Public users and non-opted-in users' data is not shared for research in identifiable form.

Guides control conservation sharing on a per-catch basis at the time of submission, from the Guide landing view. You can opt any individual catch in or out of conservation sharing before it is uploaded.

### For legal and safety reasons

We may disclose information when we believe it is necessary to comply with law, respond to valid legal process, protect the rights or safety of users or the public, or investigate suspected violations of our Acceptable Use Policy.

### In a business transfer

If Mad Thinker is involved in a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, your information may be transferred as part of that transaction, subject to continued protection under this Policy or equivalent terms.

## 5. Machine Learning and AI Processing

The Service uses machine-learning models to analyze photos you capture and estimate attributes such as species, length, and sex of a fish. These models run on your device. The inputs (your photos) are not sent to third-party AI providers. The inference results, plus a derived feature vector, are uploaded with your catch report so we can improve the models and so your community or research partners can use them.

**Model outputs are estimates, not authoritative identifications.** You can correct any AI-generated value in-app before submitting. Where accuracy matters (for example, legal size verification or scientific reporting), you should verify measurements yourself.

We may use anonymized photos and feature vectors to improve our models. Public users can opt out of model-improvement use at Settings → Privacy → ML Training Data. For guides accessing the Service through a lodge, your per-catch conservation-sharing choice also governs whether that catch's data is used to improve our models; catches you exclude from conservation sharing are also excluded from model-improvement use.

## 6. Your Rights and Choices

### Access, correction, and deletion

You have the right to access, correct, and delete personal information we hold about you.

- **In-app account deletion:** You may delete your account and associated data directly in the app at Settings → Account → Delete My Account. Deletion is permanent and will be completed within 30 days. Some information may be retained longer where required by law (for example, financial records for lodges that have paid license fees). If you access the Service through a lodge or conservation agency, deleting your account removes your access; your submitted catch data owned by the organization is handled per our agreement with them.
- **Correction:** You may correct your profile and submitted reports in the app, or contact us for fields that aren't self-editable.
- **Data access:** You may request a copy of personal information we hold about you by emailing support@madthinkertech.com.

We will respond to rights requests within 30 days. We may decline requests that are unreasonable, excessive, prohibited by law, that would affect the privacy of another user, or where we cannot authenticate you.

### Sharing preferences

- **Guides — conservation sharing:** per-catch control from the Guide landing view at the time of submission. Each catch can be individually opted in or out before upload.
- **Public users — ML training data use:** opt out at Settings → Privacy → ML Training Data. Opting out prevents your anonymized catches from being used to improve our species and length estimation models.

### Communication preferences

You can unsubscribe from promotional emails by following the directions in any message. Transactional communications (sign-in, security alerts, subscription changes) continue regardless.

### Location

You can disable location access for the app at any time in your device's Settings. Disabling location prevents location-stamped catch reports from being created.

## 7. Data Retention

We retain your information for as long as your account is active and as needed to provide the Service. After you delete your account:

- **Account credentials and profile:** deleted within 30 days.
- **Catches, photos, voice notes, and observations:** deleted within 30 days unless retained by a lodge or conservation agency that owns the data under their agreement with us.
- **Aggregated and anonymized data:** retained indefinitely because it can no longer identify you.
- **Financial records and legal records:** retained for the period required by applicable law (generally 7 years in the United States).
- **Log data and diagnostic data:** retained for up to 13 months.

## 8. Subscriptions and Payments

Public subscriptions are processed through Apple In-App Purchase. ### Lodge and conservation agency licenses

Lodges and conservation agencies pay Mad Thinker directly under separate written agreements. Individual guides, anglers, and researchers provisioned through those organizations do not pay Mad Thinker for access; their access is governed by their agreement with the organization.
